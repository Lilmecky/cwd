Scrolling Through the Day

What teen screen time data shows about habits, attention, and mental health.

Explore the Data

Introduction

Data helps make everyday behavior visible. Screen use is something many people experience, but data helps show how widespread these habits have become.

Using CDC and AAP information, this project explores teen screen use through a visual data story.

How Much Screen Time?

Over half of teens reported 4 or more hours of daily screen time.

Source: CDC/NCHS Data Brief No. 513

  • Less than 1 hour: 3.0%
  • 1 hour: 6.1%
  • 2 hours: 17.8%
  • 3 hours: 22.8%
  • 4+ hours: 50.4%

Who Reports Higher Screen Use?

Screen use differs across groups.

Source: CDC/NCHS Data Brief No. 513

Age

  • Ages 12–14: 45.6%
  • Ages 15–17: 55.0%

Gender

  • Boys: 48.3%
  • Girls: 52.5%

Screen Time and Mental Health

Higher screen use is associated with higher reported anxiety and depression symptoms.

Source: CDC/NCHS Data Brief No. 513

Anxiety Symptoms

  • Under 4 hours: 12.3%
  • 4+ hours: 27.1%

Depression Symptoms

  • Under 4 hours: 9.5%
  • 4+ hours: 25.9%

What the Data Cannot Fully Show

Screen time is not only about hours. It also depends on what people are doing online and what that use may be replacing.

The data shows patterns, but not every personal experience.
